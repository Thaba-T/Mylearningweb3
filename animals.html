ANIMALS MAP PLACES

ELEPHANT

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth, known for their intelligence, strong social bonds, and incredible memory.

GEMSBOK

The gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is a striking antelope native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly the Kalahari and Namib Deserts

GIRAFFE

The giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis) is the tallest land animal on Earth, reaching heights of up to 5.5 meters (18 feet).

GORILLA

Gorillas are the largest primates and one of our closest relatives, sharing 98.3% of their DNA with humans.

KOALA

Koalas are tree-dwelling marsupials native to Australia, often mistakenly called "koala bears" despite not being bears at all.

Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) is one of the most iconic big cats, often called the "king of the jungle."

MONKEY

Dusky leaf monkeys are arboreal, spending most of their time in trees, and they primarily feed on leaves, fruits, and flowers.

PANDA

The giant panda is a bear species native to China, known for its black-and-white fur and adorable.

TIGER

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest big cat and one of the most powerful predators on Earth.

Warthog

The warthog is a wild pig native to sub-Saharan Africa, known for its distinctive facial "warts" and curved tusks
